# MENA Market Optimizer Skill

## Purpose
Optimize websites for the broader Middle East and North Africa (MENA) region, understanding regional differences, consumer behavior, and cross-border SEO strategies.

## Activation
Use when you need to:
- Expand reach beyond Jordan to MENA region
- Understand regional market differences
- Optimize for multiple Arab countries
- Adapt content for different dialects
- Target GCC, Levant, or North African markets

## MENA Region Overview

|------------|-----------|------------|-----------|------------------|
| **GCC** | Saudi Arabia, UAE, Kuwait, Qatar, Bahrain, Oman | 60M | Very High | Very High |
| **Levant** | Jordan, Lebanon, Syria, Palestine, Iraq | 80M | Medium | Medium-High |
| **North Africa** | Egypt, Morocco, Tunisia, Algeria, Libya | 200M+ | Medium-Low | Medium |

### Market Characteristics by Region

#### Gulf Cooperation Council (GCC)
```markdown
Key Markets: Saudi Arabia, UAE

Characteristics:
- Highest purchasing power in MENA
- Very brand-conscious
- Early technology adopters
- High smartphone penetration (>90%)
- Strong preference for premium services
- English widely used alongside Arabic
- Expat populations significant
- E-commerce mature

SEO Implications:
- Both Arabic and English content valuable
- Premium positioning works
- High competition for keywords
- Strong local SEO (especially UAE)

```

#### Levant Region
```markdown
Key Markets: Jordan, Lebanon, Palestine

Characteristics:
- Well-educated population
- Price-conscious but brand-aware
- Strong digital skills
- Growing startup ecosystem
- Emigration creates diaspora audience
- Economic challenges but adaptable
- Strong community ties

SEO Implications:
- Value proposition must be clear
- Price transparency appreciated
- Local trust signals important
- Community recommendations matter
- Diaspora can be audience segment
```

#### Egypt & North Africa
```markdown
Key Markets: Egypt (largest), Morocco

Characteristics:
- Massive population (Egypt: 100M+)
- Lower purchasing power but volume
- Mobile-first (feature phones still exist)
- Local payment preferences
- Strong local competition

```

## Multi-Country SEO Strategy

### Domain Strategy

```markdown
Options:

1. Country-Code TLDs (ccTLDs)
   - example.jo (Jordan)
   - example.ae (UAE)
   - example.sa (Saudi Arabia)
   ✅ Best for single-country focus
   ❌ Expensive to maintain multiple

2. Subdirectories
   - example.com/jo/
   - example.com/ae/
   - example.com/sa/
   ✅ Consolidates domain authority
   ✅ Easier to manage
   ❌ Less geo-targeting signal

3. Subdomains
   - jo.example.com
   - ae.example.com
   ✅ Clear separation
   ❌ May split authority
   ❌ Complex management

Recommended for Jordan-based business expanding:
- Keep .com or .jo for primary
- Use subdirectories for other countries
- Implement proper hreflang
```

### hreflang Implementation for MENA

```html
<!-- For Arabic content targeting different countries -->
<link rel="alternate" hreflang="ar-JO" href="https://example.com/jo/">
<link rel="alternate" hreflang="ar-SA" href="https://example.com/sa/">
<link rel="alternate" hreflang="ar-AE" href="https://example.com/ae/">
<link rel="alternate" hreflang="ar-EG" href="https://example.com/eg/">
<link rel="alternate" hreflang="ar" href="https://example.com/"> <!-- Generic Arabic -->
<link rel="alternate" hreflang="x-default" href="https://example.com/">

<!-- For bilingual sites -->
<link rel="alternate" hreflang="en-AE" href="https://example.com/en-ae/">
```

### Country-Specific Content Adaptation

#### Saudi Arabia

```markdown
Key Differences from Jordan:

Keywords:
- "جوال" not "موبايل" (mobile)
- "الحين" not "هلق" (now)
- "وش" not "شو" (what)

Currency:
- SAR (Saudi Riyal) not JOD
- Prices in ريال سعودي

Business Hours:
- Different weekend (Fri-Sat)
- Prayer time closures
- Ramadan hours vary

Trust Signals:
- Saudi Commission for Health Specialties
- Ministry of Health registration
- Saudi Board certification
```

#### UAE

```markdown
Key Differences from Jordan:

Keywords:
- Mix of Gulf and general Arabic
- High English search volume
- Many expat-focused searches

Currency:
- AED (Dirham)

Market:
- Dubai vs Abu Dhabi distinction
- Many free zone businesses
- Expat-focused services common

Trust Signals:
- DHA (Dubai Health Authority) for Dubai
- HAAD (Abu Dhabi) for Abu Dhabi
- MOH for federal
```

#### Egypt

```markdown
Key Differences from Jordan:

Keywords:
- عايز not بدي (want)
- إزاي not كيف (how)
- دلوقتي not هلق (now)
- كويس not منيح (good)

Currency:
- EGP (Egyptian Pound)
- Price sensitivity very high

Market:
- Cairo dominates
- Massive population
- Strong local competitors
- فيسبوك (Facebook) very important

Trust Signals:
- Egyptian Medical Syndicate
- Ministry of Health Egypt
```

## Cultural Customization

### Religious Observances

```markdown
Ramadan:
- Adjust content calendar
- Ramadan-specific promotions
- Changed business hours
- Iftar/Suhoor timing content
- Religious greetings appropriate

Hajj Season:
- Saudi traffic patterns change
- Healthcare travel considerations

Eid Celebrations:
- Eid greetings in content
- Holiday closures communicated
- Promotional opportunities
```

### Visual Content Adaptation

```markdown
Image Considerations:
- Modest dress in photos
- Gender separation where appropriate
- No inappropriate gestures
- Cultural landmarks resonate
- Local faces, not Western stock photos

Video:
- Arabic subtitles essential
- Voiceover preferences vary
- Shorter attention spans on mobile
```

### Payment and Pricing

```markdown
Price Display:
- Show local currency
- Include VAT/tax information
- Payment methods vary:
  - Credit cards (GCC: high, Egypt: lower)
  - Cash on delivery (Egypt: very common)
  - Mobile wallets growing
  - Mada (Saudi), Apple Pay (UAE)

Pricing Strategy:
- GCC: Premium pricing acceptable
- Levant: Value positioning important
- Egypt: Volume/competitive pricing
```

## MENA Search Engine Optimization

### Google Dominance

```markdown
Google Market Share:

- No significant Bing/Yahoo presence
- Yandex not relevant

Google Variations:
- google.jo (Jordan)
- google.ae (UAE)
- google.com.sa (Saudi)

Local factors: As per local SEO best practices
```

```markdown
High Priority:
1. Mobile experience (97% mobile users)
2. Page speed (variable connectivity)
3. Arabic content quality
4. Local trust signals
5. E-E-A-T for YMYL topics
6. Local backlinks

MENA-Specific:
- Content freshness (recency bias)
- Social signals (Facebook, Instagram)
- Local citations
- Reviews (Google, Facebook)
```
