# E-E-A-T Content Benchmark

## Overview

E-E-A-T stands for **Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness**. This framework is used by Google's Search Quality Raters to evaluate content quality.

> "Trust is the most important member of the E-E-A-T family because untrustworthy pages have low E-E-A-T no matter how Experienced, Expert, or Authoritative they may seem." — Google

## The Four Pillars

### Experience (First "E")
**Definition**: First-hand or life experience with the topic

| Signal | Weight | How to Demonstrate |
|--------|--------|-------------------|
| Personal use/testing | High | "I've used this product for 3 months..." |
| Before/after results | High | Show real results with photos/data |
| Case studies | High | Document real patient/client experiences |
| Process documentation | Medium | "Here's how I performed the procedure..." |
| Original photos/videos | Medium | Real, not stock images |

### Expertise (Second "E")
**Definition**: Knowledge and skill in a particular field

| Signal | Weight | How to Demonstrate |
|--------|--------|-------------------|
| Formal credentials | High | Degrees, certifications, licenses |
| Professional experience | High | Years of practice, positions held |
| Published work | Medium | Articles, research, books |
| Speaking/teaching | Medium | Conferences, university positions |
| Industry recognition | Medium | Awards, fellowships |
| Specialized training | Medium | Courses, continuing education |

### Authoritativeness
**Definition**: Reputation as a go-to source in the field

| Signal | Weight | How to Demonstrate |
|--------|--------|-------------------|
| Industry citations | High | Other experts reference your work |
| Media mentions | High | Quoted in news, publications |
| Wikipedia/Wikidata | High | Verifiable notable entity |
| Professional memberships | Medium | Associations, societies |
| Backlinks from authorities | Medium | .gov, .edu, industry sites |
| Social proof | Medium | Large following, engagement |

### Trustworthiness
**Definition**: The extent to which content can be relied upon

| Signal | Weight | How to Demonstrate |
|--------|--------|-------------------|
| Accuracy | Critical | Fact-checked, correct information |
| Transparency | Critical | Clear about who you are |
| Contact information | High | Verifiable address, phone |
| Privacy/security | High | HTTPS, privacy policy |
| Source citations | Medium | Reference authoritative sources |
| Update frequency | Medium | Current, maintained content |
| Review responses | Medium | Engaged with feedback |

## YMYL (Your Money or Your Life) Topics

For topics that can impact health, safety, or financial well-being, E-E-A-T requirements are HIGHEST.

### YMYL Categories
- **Health/Medical**: Symptoms, treatments, medications
- **Financial**: Investments, taxes, loans, banking
- **Legal**: Rights, contracts, legal advice
- **Safety**: Product safety, emergency information
- **News**: Current events affecting people's lives
- **Shopping**: Major purchases (electronics, vehicles)

### Medical Website E-E-A-T Requirements

| Requirement | Must Have |
|-------------|-----------|
| Author credentials | Medical degree, specialty, license |
| Medical review | Content reviewed by qualified professional |
| Credentials displayed | Board certifications visible |
| Affiliation | Hospital or medical institution ties |
| Accuracy | Medically accurate, up-to-date |
| Sources | Citations to medical literature |
| Disclaimers | Medical advice disclaimers |

## E-E-A-T Audit Checklist

### Experience Signals
```
□ First-hand experience demonstrated
□ "I tested", "I used", "In my experience"
□ Original photos (not stock)
□ Before/after documentation
□ Case studies or examples
□ Process/methodology shared
□ Results/outcomes shown
```

### Expertise Signals
```
□ Author name and bio present
□ Credentials listed (degrees, certifications)
□ Years of experience stated
□ Professional title displayed
□ Specialized training mentioned
□ Published work referenced
□ "About the Author" section
```

### Authoritativeness Signals
```
□ Professional affiliations listed
□ Industry memberships displayed
□ Awards/recognition mentioned
□ Media appearances linked
□ Speaking engagements noted
□ Citations from other experts
□ Wikipedia/notable sources mention
```

### Trustworthiness Signals
```
□ Contact information visible
□ Physical address displayed
□ Phone number provided
□ HTTPS enabled
□ Privacy policy present
□ Terms of service available
□ Content dated/updated
□ Sources cited
□ Review responses present
□ Clear correction policy
```

## E-E-A-T for Arabic Medical Sites

### Arabic-Specific Trust Signals
```
□ Arabic credentials explained
□ Local certifications (Jordan Medical License)
□ Regional hospital affiliations
□ Local association memberships
□ Arabic patient testimonials
□ Local contact methods (WhatsApp)
□ Local currency pricing
□ Regional recognition
```

### Content Quality for Arabic
```
□ Native Arabic content (not translated)
□ Appropriate dialect for audience
□ Medical terms explained in Arabic
□ Cultural sensitivity maintained
□ Religious considerations respected
```
